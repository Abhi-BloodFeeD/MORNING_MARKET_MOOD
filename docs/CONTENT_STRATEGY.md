# Content Strategy

## Overview
This document outlines the content approach for the Market Bias Predict platform, ensuring consistency in tone, style, and purpose across all pages and components.

## Goals
1. Educate users on market-moving events and their implications
2. Provide clear, actionable insights without financial advice
3. Build trust through transparency and data-driven analysis
4. Engage users with concise, scannable content

## Audience
- Primary: Active investors, traders, finance professionals (25-55)
- Secondary: Finance enthusiasts, economics students, curious professionals
- Tertiary: General public seeking to understand market movements

## Tone & Voice
- **Authoritative yet approachable**: Expertise without jargon overload
- **Objective & neutral**: Present facts and analysis without bias
- **Concise & scannable**: Respect users' time with clear hierarchy
- **Professional & premium**: Match the quality of Bloomberg Terminal or FT
- **Empowering**: Focus on enabling understanding, not predicting with certainty

### Voice Characteristics
- Clear: Simple language, avoid unnecessary complexity
- Precise: Use accurate financial terminology when needed, with explanations
- Confident: State what is known, acknowledge uncertainty
- Engaging: Use active voice and varied sentence structure
- Trustworthy: Cite sources, show methodology, acknowledge limitations

## Content Types

### 1. Market Bias Summary (Dashboard)
- **Format**: 2-3 sentence paragraph + key metrics
- **Purpose**: Explain today's market story in plain language
- **Elements**:
  - What happened: Key events from last 24h
  - Why it matters: Connection to market movement
  - What to watch: Key levels or events ahead
- **Example**: "Markets rallied on cooler-than-expected inflation data, increasing expectations for Fed rate cuts. Technology and growth stocks led gains while energy lagged. Watch for tomorrow's retail sales data for confirmation of consumer resilience."

### 2. Event Descriptions (Economic Events & Corporate News)
- **Format**: 1-2 sentence summary + impact assessment
- **Structure**:
  - Headline: Concise, factual (max 80 characters)
  - Summary: What occurred (max 200 characters)
  - Impact: How it affects markets/sectors (max 150 characters)
  - Importance: Score 1-5 (based on potential market movability)
  - Sentiment: Bullish/Bearish/Neutral with explanation
- **Guidelines**:
  - Avoid sensationalism
  - Focus on market implications, not just the event
  - Use consistent terminology

### 3. Sector Analysis
- **Format**: Overview + top drivers + affected companies
- **Elements**:
  - Sector bias (bullish/bearish/neutral)
  - Top 3 drivers (bullet points)
  - Top 5 affected companies (with logos and brief notes)
  - Outlook: Short-term vs long-term
- **Length**: Aim for 300-500 words per sector page

### 4. Educational Content (About, Help, etc.)
- **Format**: Explainer style with examples
- **Principles**:
  - Start with the user's goal
  - Use analogies sparingly and appropriately
  - Define terms on first use
  - Progress from simple to complex
  - Include visual aids where helpful

### 5. Legal & Policy Content
- **Format**: Clear, concise, comprehensive
- **Principles**:
  - Use plain language summaries where possible
  - Provide full legal text for compliance
  - Highlight key sections (data usage, user rights)
  - Update regularly and indicate version date

## Content Structure & Formatting

### Headings
- Use sentence case (only first word and proper nouns capitalized)
- Keep under 60 characters when possible
- Make them informative and benefit-driven

### Paragraphs
- 2-4 sentences maximum
- One idea per paragraph
- Use transitions for flow

### Lists
- Use bullet points for non-sequential items
- Use numbered lists for steps or rankings
- Keep list items concise (1-2 lines)
- Parallel structure in list items

### Numbers & Data
- Spell out numbers one to nine, use numerals for 10+
- Use commas in large numbers (1,000,000)
- Format percentages with one decimal place (unless whole number)
- Currency: USD unless specified, use $ symbol
- Large numbers: abbreviate with K, M, B (e.g., $1.2M) in tight spaces

### Dates & Times
- Format: "Jun 15, 2026" or "June 15, 2026" for full date
- Time: 24-hour format for precision (e.g., 14:30 EST) or 12-hour with timezone
- Relative time only for very recent events ("2 hours ago") with exact timestamp available on hover

### Financial Terminology
- Define acronyms on first use (FOMC, CPI, PMI, etc.)
- Use standard financial definitions
- Clarify when terms have specific meanings in context

## Localization & Internationalization
- Primary language: English (US)
- Consider future localization for major markets (EU, Asia)
- Use i18n framework from the start
- Dates, numbers, currency follow locale
- Content translation: maintain tone and precision

## SEO Guidelines

### Keyword Targeting
- Primary: "market bias", "economic news analysis", "stock market sentiment"
- Secondary: "today's market outlook", "financial news impact", "sector analysis"
- Long-tail: "how does inflation affect stocks", "what drives market movement today"

### On-Page Elements
- Title Tag: `< 60 characters, include primary keyword and brand`
- Meta Description: `< 160 characters, compelling summary with CTA`
- H1: Unique per page, includes primary keyword
- URL: Short, descriptive, includes keyword
- Image Alt: Descriptive, includes context (not just "chart")
- Internal Link: Use descriptive anchor text

### Structured Data
- Implement Article schema for news/event content
- Implement FAQSchema for educational content
- Implement BreadcrumbList for navigation
- Implement Organization schema for site-wide
- Implement WebPage schema for all pages

## Content Maintenance
- Establish update frequency: Daily for dashboard/events, quarterly for educational
- Assign content owners for each section
- Implement content review process (accuracy, tone, compliance)
- Archive outdated content with redirects
- Monitor user engagement metrics (time on page, bounce rate, scroll depth)

## Content Sources & Ethics
- Primary sources: Official releases, primary data (when possible)
- Secondary sources: Reputable financial news wires (Reuters, Bloomberg, AP)
- Always attribute sources
- Clearly distinguish between factual reporting and analysis
- Disclaimers: "This is not financial advice" where appropriate
- Avoid conflicts of interest in content creation
- Corrections: Promptly acknowledge and correct errors

## Examples

### Dashboard Summary (Good)
> "Stocks rose on declining inflation pressures, boosting expectations for easier monetary policy. The S&P 500 gained 1.2% as technology shares led advances. Watch for consumer confidence data tomorrow to gauge spending resilience."

### Event Description (Good)
> **Headline**: US CPI May 2026 comes in at 2.8% YoY vs 3.0% expected
> **Summary**: Consumer prices increased less than forecast, driven by falling energy costs and goods deflation.
> **Market Impact**: Increases probability of Fed rate cuts in Q3, boosting growth stocks and reducing dollar strength.
> **Importance**: 5/5 (High-impact economic indicator)
> **Sentiment**: Bullish for stocks/bonds, bearish for USD

### Sector Analysis Snippet (Good)
> **Technology Sector: Bullish**
> **Top Drivers**: 1) Lower interest rate expectations boost growth valuations 2) Strong earnings outlook from semis 3) AI investment continues apace
> **Affected Companies**: NVDA (AI chips), MSFT (cloud), AAPL (consumer tech), GOOGL (ads), TSMC (fabrication)
> **Outlook**: Near-term volatility around earnings; long-term strength from digital transformation trends
